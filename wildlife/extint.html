Endangered Animals

Tiger

The tiger is an endangered species, facing threats such as habitat loss and poaching.

Amur Leopard

The Amur Leopard is one of the most critically endangered big cats, characterized by its beautiful spotted coat and remarkable agility, with less than 100 individuals remaining in the wild.

Philippine Eagle

The Philippine Eagle, also known as the Monkey-eating Eagle, is a critically endangered bird endemic to the Philippines, with a remarkable wingspan and considered one of the largest and most powerful eagles in the world.

Golden Poison Frog

The Golden Poison Frog is a brightly colored and highly toxic amphibian, endemic to Colombia, facing severe habitat loss and illegal trafficking due to its vibrant appearance and toxicity.

Mountain Gorilla

The Mountain Gorilla is a critically endangered primate species found in the dense forests of Central Africa, known for its gentle nature and tight-knit social groups, facing habitat degradation and illegal hunting.

Black Rhinoceros

The Black Rhinoceros is a critically endangered mammal known for its distinctive hooked upper lip and solitary nature, facing threats from poaching and habitat loss.

Radiated Tortoise

The Radiated Tortoise is a critically endangered species found in Madagascar, known for its striking star-patterned shell and slow movement, threatened by habitat loss and illegal pet trade.

Hawksbill Turtle

The Hawksbill Turtle is a critically endangered sea turtle known for its beautiful patterned shell and is vital to maintaining the health of coral reefs, facing threats from habitat degradation, pollution, and illegal trade.
